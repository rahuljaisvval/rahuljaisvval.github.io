Aurora venues - Responsive website (dummy project)

Aurora venues are a chain of event venues that provide venues for exquisite weddings, birthdays, anniversarys, corporate and other special events. They needed an appealing and easy to use website that showcases their venues and makes it easy for the user to reach them.

The Problem and The Goal

Aurora venues need a simple yet appealing website which exutes the aurora venues brand to the potential users and giving them a way to reach them. It should be usable accross all the devices.

So the goal is to design a user-friendly responsive website that will help aurora venues showcase their venues and help users reach them.

My Role and Responsibilities

UX designer, leading the responsive website design from conception to delivery.

Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, iterating on designs, determining information architecture, and responsive design.

Aurora venues - Responsive website

Welcome to Aurora Venues. A responsive website that is easy to use, visually appealing, and informative. We understand that planning an event can be overwhelming, which is why we designed our website to provide a seamless experience. You can easily navigate through our pages to learn about services, explore our venue and the events we have organised, and book your event.

Initial ideation

Taking the time to draft iterations of each screen of the website on paper ensured that the elements that made it to digital wireframes would be well-suited to address user pain points. For the home screen, I prioritized a quick and easy selection process to help users save time.

Digital Wireframes

After ideating and drafting some paper wireframes, I created the initial designs for the Aurora venues - Website. These designs focused on delivering a simple to use clutter free design that can be easily used with talkback.

Low Fidelity Prototype

To prepare for usability testing, I created a low-fidelity prototype that connected all the screens.

  • Try yourself

Usability study: findings

These were the main findings uncovered by the usability study:

  • People want clutter free user interface .
  • People wanted more contact options.
  • Many users pointed out they wished they could view event specific images.

Changes after usability study:

Based on the insights from the usability study, I applied design changes like providing clearer indication navigating through the app. Making contact button accesible from every page and addinf more contact options in the contact page.
Additional design changes includes a more event specific image gallery page.

High Fidelity Prototypes

The high-fidelity prototype followed the same user flow as the low-fidelity prototype, including design changes made after the usability study. The design focused on visual appeal which complements the brand identity and is aesthetically pleasing.

Takeaways

Prioritizing user experience: Designing a user-friendly and intuitive website is crucial for helping potential clients navigate through the information they need and to book events with ease.

User Research on potential clients: To understand their unique needs and goals for the website is important for creating a design that aligns with their needs and goals.

Focusing on visual appeal: Designing an aesthetically pleasing website that showcases the beauty and functionality of the venue, with the brand's identity in mind helps attract potential clients and leave a lasting impression.

Staying up-to-date with technology and trends: Keeping up with the latest technology and design trends helps ensure that the event venue website stays current and relevant to potential clients.

Continuously testing and iterating: Conducting user testing and gathering feedback on the website helps identify areas for improvement and ensure that the website is meeting the needs of the venue and its clients.
